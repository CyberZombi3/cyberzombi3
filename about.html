About

Who I am and what I spend my time building.

> Who I Am

I'm Aaron Moorcroft, known online as CyberZombi3. I'm a security researcher and software engineer with a habit of building things from first principles rather than just using them off the shelf — whether that's writing an operating system's bootloader by hand, or wiring up a local AI agent instead of relying on a cloud API.

My work sits at the intersection of offensive security, systems programming, and applied AI. I care about understanding how a system actually works underneath — down to the protected-mode transition or the raw Nmap output — and I build tools to make that understanding faster to reach the next time.

> What I Do

Offensive security tooling — automation around reconnaissance and enumeration (Nmap wrappers, autonomous pentest agents) built to remove the repetitive parts of a pentest so more time goes into the interesting parts.

Systems programming — low-level work including a custom x86 operating system written from scratch: bootloader, protected mode, graphics, and networking.

Blue team / detection engineering — a self-hosted SOC lab combining a SIEM, IDS, honeypot, and threat-intel stack to practice detection and incident response from the defender's side.

Applied AI & agents — local, privacy-respecting AI assistants and multi-agent pipelines that plan, execute, and review their own work.

iOS development — SwiftUI apps and games, usually with a cyberpunk aesthetic and a lot of attention paid to feel and feedback (haptics, sound, animation).

> Background

I come from a hands-on offensive security background, working through real-world style labs and structured training to build practical penetration testing skills, which now feeds directly into the tools I build (see the AI Pentest Agent and NmapEnhanced).
